# 1. Executive Verdict

## PARTIAL RESTRUCTURE REQUIRED

The core positioning is correct and must not change. "PP-Rollenetiketten für Produktmarken, die regelmäßig nachbestellen" is the right strategic premise. The product catalog, pricing structure, and configurator are essentially correct.

What is missing is not strategy — it is execution depth and credibility infrastructure.

- **The site is not Google Ads ready.** No GA4, no conversion tag, no Consent Mode v2. Every ad click is invisible. This is the single most dangerous gap.
- **The homepage title and hero copy are directionally correct but lack specificity signals** that separate Labelpilot from generalist print shops above the fold.
- **The trust layer is skeleton-level.** No seller transparency, no DDP framing, no verifiable claims. A German B2B buyer who googles will find a Hong Kong entity with no reassurance.
- **The SEO page set is partially built** but the highest-priority commercial keyword pages (/rollenetiketten, /rollenetiketten-drucken, /lebensmitteletiketten, /flaschenetiketten-drucken) are either missing dedicated pages or lack the content depth to outrank typographus.de (AS 27) and aufkleberdrucker24.de (AS 33).
- **The reorder system is built but invisible to first-time visitors.** The value proposition of saved artwork + 30-second reorder is buried — it is the primary differentiator and should be above the fold.
- **Feature flags are suppressing revenue.** ADDONS flag off = Design Service (€40), Express (€9.90), and the "upload your own data" path are hidden from every visitor.
- **The navigation is weak for SEO signal and buyer orientation.** "Materialien" and "Branchen" are internal labels, not buyer-intent words. A B2B buyer landing from a search ad does not immediately see "Lebensmitteletiketten" or "Rollenetiketten" in the nav.
- **B2C sticker language has not been fully purged.** Some page copy and meta descriptions retain generic print shop tone.
- **No Musterbox prominence.** The sample box is a conversion moat for B2B buyers who are not ready to commit — it should be visible in the nav and hero.
- **Delivery time is vague.** "ca. 10–14 Werktage nach Ihrer Freigabe" with a "keine bindende Garantie" note destroys confidence for B2B planners.

> **Labelpilot.de — PP-Rollenetiketten für Produktmarken mit wiederkehrenden Auflagen.**
> Für Lebensmittel, Getränke und Supplemente. Klare Preise, geprüfte Druckdaten, schnelle Nachbestellung.

## English (internal reference only)

> Labelpilot.de is a Germany-focused B2B PP roll-label ordering and reorder platform for food, beverage, supplement, and packaged-goods micro-brands. It is not a generic print shop, sticker shop, or broad online druckerei.

# 9. Copy Rules for German Public Pages

## Allowed Tone

- Direct, specific, technical but not cold
- Confident without superlatives
- B2B professional — addresses "Sie" or uses nominative framing ("Ihre Marke", "Ihr Artwork", "Ihre Produktion")
- Honest about production origin, delivery, and entity
- Functional — every sentence earns its place
- Monochromatic brand voice: serious, focused, no humor, no playfulness

## Banned Tone

- Generic partnership language ("Ihr zuverlässiger Partner für...")
- Superlatives without evidence ("die besten Etiketten", "höchste Qualität")
- Consumer/hobbyist framing ("perfekt für Events", "für Hochzeiten und Feste")
- German startup clichés ("transparent", "nachhaltig", "auf Augenhöhe")
- English buzzwords on public pages ("premium quality", "seamless experience", "best-in-class")
- Cheap/low-price anchoring ("günstig", "billig", "zum Niedrigpreis")
- Fake German production claims ("Hergestellt in Deutschland" when production is in Turkey)
- SaaS jargon ("Onboarding", "Workflow", "Touchpoint", "Go-to-market")

## Banned Phrases (Specific)

| Phrase | Why Banned |
|---|---|
| "Ihr zuverlässiger Partner für Drucklösungen" | Generic, unverifiable, sounds like a printer catalog |
| "Höchste Druckqualität" | Superlative without specification |
| "Made in Germany" | Production is in Turkey — direct lie |
| "Günstige Etiketten" | Cheap-price anchoring that conflicts with B2B positioning |
| "Für alle Anlässe" | B2C/hobbyist framing |
| "Sticker" | Not our product category — signals sticker shop |
| "Ab 10 Stück" | Wrong quantity signal — implies hobbyist, not B2B |
| "Einfach online gestalten" | Suggests DIY design tool, which we do not have yet |
| "Trusted partner" | English + generic |
| "schnell und einfach" | Hollow, overused by every print shop |
| "transparente Preise" | This is Transparent PP label copy — never use "transparent" as a price descriptor on the same page |

## CTA Rules

- Primary CTAs: action-first German verbs. "Jetzt konfigurieren" ✓ "Preis berechnen" ✓ "Musterbox anfordern" ✓
- Secondary CTAs: qualify the action. "Angebot anfordern" + subtext "für 20.000+ Stück oder Sonderanforderungen" ✓
- No "Klick hier" — always state what happens after the click
- No "Mehr erfahren" as a standalone CTA — append what they will learn: "Mehr über die Nachbestellung erfahren"
- No "Kontaktieren Sie uns" as primary CTA — too passive for commercial intent pages

## Paragraph Length Rules

- Max 2 desktop lines per paragraph on product/landing pages
- No wall of text above the fold — ever
- Use bullet lists for specifications, inclusions, and use cases
- Use tables for: pricing, spec data, material comparison, inclusion/exclusion
- Use accordion for FAQ
- Keep FAQ answers under 4 lines each

## German-Only Rule

All customer-facing content is German. No exceptions.
- CTAs: German
- Error messages: German
- FAQ: German
- Metadata (title/description): German
- Schema markup: values in German
- Eyebrows and labels: German (Ausnahme: technical abbreviations like "CMYK", "PDF", "DPI" are acceptable)
- Admin internals may be English

## Production/Seller Transparency Rule

Every product page and landing page must have a visible disclosure:
> "Produktion in der Türkei. Lieferung DDP nach Deutschland — Zoll und Import inklusive. Zahlungsabwicklung über Stripe. Vertragspartner: [Entity]."

This is not optional. It is the proactive trust answer to the question every German B2B buyer will ask when they see the HK Impressum.

## No Competitor Name Rule

Competitor names never appear in public-facing copy, URLs, meta descriptions, or schema markup.
Study their structure (product depth, pricing clarity, spec tables). Never name them.
