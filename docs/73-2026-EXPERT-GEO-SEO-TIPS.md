# Labelpilot.de — 2026 Expert GEO and SEO Rules

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the 2026 SEO + GEO rule system for **Labelpilot.de**.

In this document:

```txt
SEO = Search Engine Optimization
GEO = Generative Engine Optimization / AI Search Visibility
```

GEO here does **not** mean only geographic SEO. It means becoming citeable, extractable and trustworthy in AI-generated answers, Google AI Overviews, Google AI Mode, ChatGPT-style search, Perplexity-style answers and future answer engines.

Labelpilot.de must act as:

> A German B2B label infrastructure platform for food, beverage, supplement and micro packaged-product brands.

Not:

> A generic online print shop.

## 3. Source-of-Truth Principles

Google’s official guidance says the fundamentals still matter for AI features:

```txt
crawlability
indexability
helpful content
internal links
page experience
textual content
high-quality images/videos where useful
structured data matching visible content
Search Console monitoring
```

Google also states there are no special technical requirements, no special AI markup and no special schema required just to appear in AI Overviews or AI Mode.

Therefore Labelpilot.de must not chase fake “AI SEO hacks”.

The correct rule is:

> Make every important page technically indexable, textually clear, entity-rich, sourceable, internally linked and commercially useful.


## 41. Source Notes

This document is based on the current Google Search Central guidance and current AI-search behavior.

Important source principles:

1. Google says SEO is about helping search engines understand content and helping users find the site through Search.
2. Google says Search Essentials make pages more likely to appear, but indexing/ranking is not guaranteed.
3. Google says AI Overviews and AI Mode use the same foundational SEO best practices; there are no additional technical requirements, no special AI schema and no special machine-readable AI file required.
4. Google says AI features can use query fan-out across related searches and supporting pages.
5. Google says structured data must match visible page content.
6. Google says pages need to be crawlable, internally linked, indexable and eligible for snippets to appear as supporting links in AI features.
7. Research on AI Overviews shows AI search can reduce traffic for some informational content, so commercial conversion and sourceability matter more than raw informational traffic.
